# Field note from Dusty — fertilizer cost down to $0.60/bu

## What Dusty shared

> "He has been cutting and are $0.60 per bu now for fertilizer."

Interpreting: Dusty's growers have been **cutting N rates** (likely in conjunction with biostim / better 4R practices) and are now running at **$0.60/bu of corn produced in fertilizer cost**.

## Why this matters — the math

Fertilizer-cost-per-bushel is the right KPI for judging rate-cut economics (better than $/ac, because it normalizes for yield).

Rough benchmarking at current Iowa corn economics (assume ~$4/bu cash corn):

| Yield | $/bu fert | $/ac fert | % of gross revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 200 bu/ac | $0.60 | $120 | 15% |
| 220 bu/ac | $0.60 | $132 | 15% |
| 240 bu/ac | $0.60 | $144 | 15% |

Historical Iowa average has been closer to **$0.80–$1.10/bu** for fertilizer on corn. **$0.60/bu is aggressive-efficient territory** — the kind of number that suggests:
- N rates cut 15–25% below MRTN
- Manure or legume credits being captured well
- Biostim or stabilizer use reducing losses
- High yields spreading fixed fert cost

## Implications for our pitch

1. **Dusty's growers already believe cuts work.** Social proof when pitching the lignin biostim program — they're not starting from "can I cut?" but from "how much more can I cut safely?"
2. **ROI framing** — a biostim that enables another $0.05–$0.10/bu cut on top is $10–$24/ac at 200–240 bu yields. Biostim product cost of $8–$15/ac clears that bar comfortably.
3. **Trial data we want back** — ask Dusty to share per-treatment fertilizer $/bu from the 320-ac trial (see prior inquiry). That's the number his peers care about.
