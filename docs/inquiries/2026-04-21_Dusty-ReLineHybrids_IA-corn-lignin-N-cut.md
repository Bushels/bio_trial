# Iowa corn — lignin biostim in planter liquid, N-rate cut question

## The scenario Dusty described

- Iowa farmer, **320 acres of corn**
- Wants to add a **lignin biostimulant** to the **liquid fertilizer at planting**
- **10 ton/ac of feedlot pack manure** applied
- Plans **sap tests at V5 and VT**
- Soil: **4.0% OM**, **19 CEC**

## Dusty's questions

1. Explain 4.0% OM
2. Explain 19 CEC
3. Based on data/research, how much could they cut their N rate using lignin biostim?
4. What's a good rate (or rate cut) to test at?

## Our answer — summary

**Soil profile interpretation**
- 4.0% OM: above Iowa average; contributes ~80–120 lb N/ac/yr from mineralization (20–30 lb N per 1% OM)
- 19 CEC: medium-high, typical silty clay loam; good NH₄⁺ retention, low leaching risk, strong buffering

**N credits before biostim**
- Feedlot pack at 10 ton: ~100–140 lb total N, **~50–70 lb available year 1**, plus ~50–80 lb P₂O₅ and ~80–120 lb K₂O
- OM bump above MRTN baseline: ~+10–15 lb/ac
- Starting synthetic N need (corn-on-corn, MRTN ~190 lb): ~**120 lb/ac** before biostim
  - Corn-after-soybean equivalent: ~90 lb/ac

**Lignin biostim mechanism + research read**
- Mild urease/nitrification slowing
- Chelation of micronutrients that drive N-assimilation enzymes (nitrate reductase, chlorophyll)
- Root architecture stimulation → bigger scavenging volume
- Microbial priming of OM and manure mineralization
- Published field data: **10–20% N-rate cuts** typical without yield loss; this grower's high-OM + manure + high-CEC profile is an ideal responder

**Recommended trial design** (randomized replicated strips, 3–4 reps)

| Treatment | Synthetic N | Biostim | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Grower check | 120 lb (100%) | No | Baseline |
| Biostim + full | 120 lb (100%) | Yes | Isolate yield bump |
| Biostim + 15% cut | 102 lb (85%) | Yes | Safe cut |
| Biostim + 25% cut | 90 lb (75%) | Yes | Aggressive cut |
| Biostim + 35% cut *(opt)* | 78 lb (65%) | Yes | Find the cliff |

**Sap/diagnostic plan**
- V5 sap NO₃-N: early-season supply check; rescue side-dress if cut strips <80% of check
- VT sap + petiole NO₃ (target ~800–1,500 ppm): predicts grain-fill N sufficiency
- Add end-of-season cornstalk nitrate (R6, lower 8") as the retrospective gold standard

## Bottom line given

Strong responder profile. **Bracket 85% and 75% of normal synthetic N** as main test points, keep a biostim+full-rate strip to separate yield bump from rate cut, let V5/VT sap steer any rescue side-dress.
