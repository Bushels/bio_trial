## 2. What we're trying to achieve

Biostimulants as a category are full of marketing and thin on data. We're trying to do the opposite — build a **farmer-run, public-by-default dataset** that lets you (and every other grower) judge whether this product is worth your money in 2027.

Specifically, we're looking for evidence — or lack of it — on four claims:

- **Stronger root systems**
- **Better drought and heat tolerance**
- **Healthier canopy and higher yield**
- **Cutting synthetic nitrogen without cutting yield**

Every cooperator's data point — including the ones that show no benefit — goes on the public scoreboard at **[trial.buperac.com/trial](https://trial.buperac.com/trial)**. Nobody edits your numbers. Nothing gets "adjusted" in post. You see the same scoreboard the company sees.

If BioLift works, the data will show it. If it doesn't, the data will show that too. Either way, you'll know.

## 4. Before the trial begins — setup details worth adding

The more the dashboard knows about your field going in, the more useful your trial record is at the end. None of this is required to get started, but every item you fill in makes the season data easier to interpret — and if we ever want to compare your field to similar fields across the program, these are the details that make that comparison honest.

When your private dashboard goes live, you can add:

**About the field itself**
- **A name you'll recognize.** "South quarter", "Home 80", "Old Henderson place" — whatever you already call it on the farm. We store this as the field nickname so every event logs against the right piece of ground.
- **Legal land description** (LSD-Sec-Twp-Rge-Mer). Never shown publicly — stored encrypted and visible only to you and the trial operators. Useful for precise identification and for any post-season mapping work.
- **Soil samples.** If you've pulled samples recently — whether from a commercial lab or your own — upload the PDF report to the field's file list. Organic matter, pH, CEC, and residual nutrient numbers all help explain why your treated-vs-check delta landed where it did.
- **Last year's crop.** Previous crop drives disease pressure, nitrogen carryover, and emergence patterns. One dropdown, big interpretive payoff.

**Seeding details (once you seed)**
- **Seed day** — the date you actually put the crop in.
- **Seeding rate** — lb/ac or seeds/ac, whichever you use.
- **Fertilizer blend and rate** — e.g. "30-20-10-5 @ 80 lb/ac in the seed row, plus 100 lb/ac urea banded." Free-text field, put it in the way you'd say it.
- **Seed treatment applied?** Yes/no, and what.

**Product plan**
- **How you're going to use the jug** — pick one per field:
  - **Seed treatment** (applied in-furrow or on seed at seeding)
  - **Foliar spray** (applied to the canopy in-season)
- **Planned application date(s).** You can log as many as you expect — and adjust them as the season goes.

**Through the season**
- **Moisture checks.** Any time you pull a moisture reading during the season — in-season crop moisture, soil moisture probe, rainfall in the gauge — log it. A dated moisture number is one of the highest-signal observations you can submit, because a good-looking canopy in a wet year reads very differently than in a dry one.

You don't have to fill it all in at once. The dashboard lets you come back and add anything later — but the sooner the baseline's in, the more context every in-season event carries.

## 5. What you have to track — and how

This is a *real* trial, so we need a few data points through the season. We've built it to take roughly **2 minutes per check-in**, not 20.

### Pick the trial design that fits your operation

When you log into your dashboard, you declare how you want to structure the trial. Five options — pick one per field. In plain English:

| Option | What it means in the field | How good the comparison is |
|---|---|---|
| **Strip trial** | You spray treated strips and leave untreated strips, alternating, in the same field. | Gold standard. Cleanest read. |
| **Split field** | Spray half the field, leave the other half untreated. | Near gold standard. |
| **Whole field vs your 3-year history** | Treat the whole field; we compare to your own past yields on that field. | Good — but weather varies year-to-year. |
| **Whole field vs a neighbour field** | Treat one field; leave a similar neighbouring field untreated as the check. | Good — as long as the two fields are genuinely similar. |
| **Observational** | Treat the whole field; no check at all. Just tell us what you see. | Lowest rigor. Still counted, but flagged as observational on the public board. |

Don't overthink it — pick what fits how you actually farm. We'd rather have an honest split-field than a pretend strip trial.

### What you actually submit, and when

**At application time** (every time you put the product out — log each one separately):
- **Date of application.** If you apply the product more than once — e.g. a seed treatment at seeding plus a foliar pass later — log a separate event for each date.
- **Field it went on.**
- **Application method** — seed treatment (in-furrow or on-seed at seeding) or foliar spray.
- **Rate (L/ac)** and tank-mix partners, if any.
- A photo is nice but not required.

**A couple of times through the season** (emergence, mid-season, any stress event):
- A photo of the treated strip
- A photo of the check (if you have one)
- What you see — typed or dictated. Use your phone's talk-to-text. Dirty-hand-friendly.
- **Moisture readings** — in-season crop moisture, soil moisture probe values, rain-gauge totals. Any number with a date attached helps explain the yield result later.
- Optional: anything unusual — heat, hail, frost, pest, herbicide damage

**At harvest** (the one we care about most):
- Yield in bu/ac for each plot (treated, check)
- A photo of the yield monitor or scale ticket
- Moisture and grade if you have them

Minimum to count as a completed trial: **one application event + one yield event + at least two in-season check-ins.** More is better, and every photo and note strengthens the dataset.

### Two ways to submit — pick whichever is easier for you

**Option A — Telegram bot (lowest friction).**
Search `@BuperacTrialBot` in Telegram, tap Start, and paste in the binding code from your dashboard (one tap). After that:
- **Type or dictate anything** — it gets logged as an observation.
- **Send a photo** — it gets uploaded and timestamped against your trial automatically.
- **Type `/apply`** — the bot pops up a list of your fields, you tap one, then tap whether it was a seed treatment or a foliar spray, and it logs an application event.
- **Type `/yield 52.3`** at harvest — tap the field, and your yield's in.

No app install. No login. Same Telegram you use for family group chats.

**Option B — Web dashboard.**
Open your private dashboard link on any browser. Add fields, add plots (for strip/split trials), fill out a form per event, drag and drop PDFs or photos. Everything the bot does is available here too, plus a full timeline of what you've logged.

Most farmers use both — bot in the truck, dashboard at the kitchen table.

## 6. How the data gets uploaded

Short version: **you hit submit, it's there.** But for the people who want to know how it actually moves:

- **Photos from Telegram** go straight from your phone → our storage → attached to the right trial event. The bot confirms "logged" inside a second or two.
- **Photos from the web dashboard** use a one-time signed upload URL. Nothing stays on the browser.
- **Text observations, application events, yield events** — each one is its own timeline entry. You can see them in your dashboard, newest first.
- **Your private data stays private.** Name, farm name, phone, email, RM, exact legal description — none of that leaves the database through the public scoreboard. The public board only knows your **province** and the **crop** you're growing.
- **Photo sharing is per-photo opt-in.** On every event you log, there's a "Show on public dashboard" checkbox. It's unchecked by default. Photos only appear publicly if you explicitly tick the box, one at a time.
- If you want off the trial completely at any point, just say so — we can anonymize everything you've submitted or remove it entirely.

Two outputs at the end of the season:

### For you, personally

Your dashboard becomes your **own trial record** — every photo, every observation, every yield number, all in one timeline. You can export it, print it, or just use it to make your own call about 2027.

### For everybody (including you)

The **public scoreboard at [trial.buperac.com/trial](https://trial.buperac.com/trial)** fills in live through the season. As harvest data comes in, per-crop yield deltas appear — but only once **at least 3 farms** have reported on the same crop, so no individual result ever identifies a grower. The scoreboard shows:

- How many acres are enrolled, how many cooperators, how many events logged
- A mix of "Controlled / Referenced / Observational" trials so you can judge the rigor behind every number
- An anonymized activity feed — *"A farmer in SK logged an application event on canola"*
- A photo wall of the shots farmers opted in to share
- Yield deltas by crop, when the data density is high enough to show them

That's the entire point. No cherry-picking. No glossy PDF six months after the fact. A live, honest scoreboard that you and every other grower in the Prairies can look at and judge for yourselves.

## 8. Common questions

**"What if my trial goes sideways — hail, drought, sprayer miss?"**
Log it. That's the gold. A failed trial with good notes is more valuable than a clean trial with no context. You don't get penalized for a bad season — we just want the honest story.

**"Do I have to use Telegram?"**
No. The web dashboard does everything the bot does. Telegram is there because most farmers already have it and it's faster in the truck.

**"Who sees my raw data?"**
You, the Buperac trial operators (who are running the program and monitoring the results), and a couple of trusted SixRing vendor accounts limited to operations only — paid status, shipping, delivery. That's it. Nobody else has direct database access. The public board reads only aggregate, anonymized, privacy-floored data.

**"Can I pull out?"**
Yes. Before the product ships — full refund minus a small admin fee. After it ships — the jug's yours; you can stop logging whenever. Data you've already submitted stays in the dataset anonymized unless you ask us to delete it.

**"What's SixRing? What's Buperac?"**
SixRing Solutions is the Alberta-based company behind the TeraCoat / TeraSix family of lignin-derived biostimulants — they make the product. **Buperac is an independent program with no affiliation to SixRing** — we're set up specifically to run and monitor this trial, collect the data honestly, and publish it without a company filter. That independence is the whole point: if the product works, the numbers show it; if it doesn't, the numbers show that too, and nobody at SixRing gets to edit the scoreboard.
